## Start With the Number That Actually Matters

If you have owned a South Florida home for several years, your equity has likely changed more than you have tracked. Most owners think in terms of sale price. The number that actually shapes your next move is *net proceeds* — what remains after commissions, closing costs, payoff, and prorations.

Whether you are considering downsizing, relocating, or simply testing the market, a thirty-minute equity review answers the questions that a sale price alone cannot:

- What would I realistically net at today's market value?
- How does that fund my next chapter — a smaller home, a move, or reinvestment?
- Is my timing working for me or against me?

You can model the first question yourself. The [South Florida Seller's Net Sheet](/south-florida-sellers-net-sheet-2026.pdf?utm_source=journal&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=review-equity&funnel_stage=bottom_funnel) shows what sellers keep across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach.

## Then Ask the Second Question

Knowing your equity is half the work. The other half is knowing how that equity gets *realized* — because a home is only worth what a qualified buyer will pay, and that depends on exposure.

> Features describe a property. Distribution determines its price.

An online estimate tells you a number. It does not tell you who will see your home, how it will be positioned against current competition, or which buyers and agents will be activated when it launches. That is the difference between a passive listing and a positioned one.

## A Three-Point Check Before You List

1. **Equity** — Know your net, not just your list price. Model it with the [net sheet](/south-florida-sellers-net-sheet-2026.pdf?utm_source=journal&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=review-equity&funnel_stage=bottom_funnel).
2. **Value** — Get a property-specific read from current MLS data, not an algorithm. Request a [confidential home value review](/home-value?utm_source=journal&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=review-equity&funnel_stage=bottom_funnel).
3. **Distribution** — Understand how the property will be exposed and positioned before it goes public.

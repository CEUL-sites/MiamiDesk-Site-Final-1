## The Homestead Portability Calculation

Florida's Save Our Homes cap limits your assessed value increases to 3% per year. Every year you've owned your Weston home, that cap has been building a gap between your assessed value and your market value. When you sell your homestead and buy another in Florida, that accumulated benefit transfers to the new property — reducing your taxable value from day one. The mechanics, deadlines, and dollar limits are covered in detail in [Florida homestead and Save Our Homes portability](/journal/florida-homestead-portability-benefits-2026).

Here is how the math works for this specific scenario:

**Step 1 — SOH differential on the Weston home**

| | |
|---|---|
| Weston just value (sale price) | $1,170,000 |
| Weston assessed value (derived from $15,735 tax bill) | $878,158 |
| **SOH differential accumulated** | **$291,842** |

**Step 2 — Portability transferred (prorated for downsize)**

| | |
|---|---|
| Formula | SOH diff × (new value ÷ old value) |
| Calculation | $291,842 × ($580,000 ÷ $1,170,000) |
| **Portability amount transferred** | **$144,674** |

**Step 3 — Boca Raton taxable value with portability**

| | Without Portability | With Portability |
|---|---|---|
| Just value (purchase price) | $580,000 | $580,000 |
| Less portability benefit | — | ($144,674) |
| Less homestead exemption | ($50,000) | ($50,000) |
| **Taxable assessed value** | **$530,000** | **$385,326** |
| Annual tax (~17 mills, Palm Beach Co.) | ~$9,010 / $751 mo | ~$6,551 / **$546 mo** |

**Portability saves $2,459 per year — $205 per month — compared to a buyer with no portable benefit. And the savings compound: the new SOH cap starts building again from this lower baseline.**

*For reference: the current owner of this Boca unit pays $8,258/year under their own SOH position. With your Weston portability applied, your starting tax would be $6,551 — lower than what they pay.*

## The Timing Matters

Portability must be applied for in the year you establish your new Florida homestead. The transfer window is fixed and cannot be recovered after it closes. That means the sale date, the purchase date, and the county filing deadline need to be coordinated before you sign a listing agreement — not after.
